The spend leader's view

SAP for a CPO

If you control what the company buys, your SAP world is "spend management" — a family of products that handles every rupee going out, from raw materials to consultants to airfare.

🛒 Your relationship with SAP

For a CPO, SAP is the governed marketplace for all company spending — every supplier, contract, purchase order and invoice in one auditable flow, so money is spent wisely and on the books.

😣 Your pain points

  • Thousands of suppliers, hard to track risk and performance
  • "Maverick spend" — people buying outside agreed contracts
  • Slow approvals and invoices that don't match purchase orders
  • Three different kinds of spend (goods, services, travel) in three silos

🧰 The SAP products in your orbit

  • SAP Ariba — the big one. Source-to-paySource-to-pay = the whole journey: find suppliers → negotiate → contract → order → receive → pay.: sourcing, supplier management, contracts, buying & invoicing.
  • SAP Fieldglass — for the external workforce: contractors, consultants, temps, SOW-based services
  • SAP Concur — travel & expense (T&E)
  • SAP Business Network — the B2B network connecting you to suppliers in real time

🤖 What's new: Autonomous Spend

The 2026 Autonomous Spend agents and Ariba's Agentic Intake Management classify spend requests, route approvals, and resolve invoice exceptions automatically — turning procurement from manual chasing into exception-based oversight.

🔗 How it connects

Every purchase ultimately becomes a financial entry. When an Ariba invoice is approved, it posts to finance in S/4HANA. When a Concur expense is reimbursed, the same. The bridge is BTP Integration Suite — procurement and finance, always in sync.
