SAP for a CIO

If finance owns the numbers, IT owns the platform they run on. The CIO makes the decisions that shape the entire SAP landscape — where it runs, how it connects, and how it stays upgradeable.

🖥️ Your relationship with SAP

For a CIO, SAP is the digital backbone of the company. Your job isn't to use it — it's to make sure it's secure, integrated, affordable, and able to evolve without breaking.

😣 Your pain points

  • The 2027 ECC deadline — you must move the legacy core to cloud, and it's a huge program
  • Dozens of systems (SAP and non-SAP) that all need to talk to each other
  • Every customization makes upgrades harder — "technical debt" piles up
  • Security, compliance, and cost all land on your desk

🧰 The SAP products in your orbit

  • SAP BTP — your platform for everything custom. App dev, automation, AI, data — all in one governed place.
  • Integration Suite — the tool that connects SAP↔SAP and SAP↔non-SAP in real time
  • Deployment strategy — you decide on-prem vs RISE vs GROW vs hybrid
  • SAP LeanIX — to map and rationalize your whole application landscape
  • SAP Cloud ALM — to run and monitor the landscape

🧹 Your governing principle: Clean Core

The single most important rule a modern CIO enforces is Clean CoreClean Core = keep the ERP standard and unmodified; put all custom work on BTP as side-by-side extensions, so upgrades stay painless.. Never modify the ERP core. All custom needs go to BTP as side-by-side extensions, bridged by the Cloud Connector. This keeps the system upgradeable forever — and it's why the BTP skill set is the #1 SAP hiring priority.

🎬 A decision in your life

Your ECC system hits end-of-support in 2027. You weigh options: RISE (keep flexibility) vs GROW (standardize). You choose RISE for the complex core, but move HR to cloud SuccessFactors and run new apps on BTP. You've just designed a hybrid landscape — the most common real-world shape.
