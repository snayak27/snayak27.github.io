SAP for a Developer

Every other role uses SAP. The developer extends it — building the custom apps, automations and AI agents that make standard SAP fit a specific business. This is the fastest-growing, most in-demand SAP role.

👨‍💻 Your relationship with SAP

For a developer, SAP is a platform to build on. The core ERP stays standard (clean core); you create everything custom beside it on BTP — apps, workflows, integrations, and now AI agents.

🧰 Your toolkit: SAP Build & BTP

  • SAP Build Apps — low-code/no-code visual app builder (formerly AppGyver). Drag-and-drop web & mobile apps.
  • SAP Build Process Automation — visual workflows + RPA (the automation engine)
  • SAP Build Work Zone — the portal/launchpad that ties apps together
  • SAP Build Code — pro-code with AI assistance (CAP, SAPUI5)
  • Joule Studio — build and govern AI agents (the 2026 frontier)
  • Integration Suite — connect everything (iFlows, APIs, events)

🧩 Low-code vs pro-code — your two paths

  • Low-code/no-code — citizen developersCitizen developer = a business user who builds apps with visual tools, no traditional coding. and fast delivery. This is the SAP Build Developer (C_LCNC) certification path.
  • Pro-code — full control with CAP, RAP (cloud ABAP), Java/Node on Cloud Foundry, or containers on Kyma.

🎬 A build in your life

Finance complains the dispute-resolution process is manual. You build a Build App with an approval screen, wire it to S/4HANA via an OData service, add a Build Process Automation workflow for approvals, and surface it in Work Zone. Two weeks, no core modification — pure clean-core extension. That's the job.

🚀 Why this role matters most

Because every landscape is hybrid and clean-core is mandatory, the people who can build the glue and extensions are the #1 SAP hiring priority. Add deep domain knowledge (like finance) on top, and you're a techno-functional BTP architect — the rarest, most valuable profile in the SAP world.
