The operations leader's view

SAP for a COO

If you run operations, your SAP world is about physical reality — planning what to make, making it, and moving it to customers. Three verbs: plan, make, move.

🏭 Your relationship with SAP

For a COO, SAP is the control tower for goods — it sees demand, balances supply, runs the factory floor, and tracks every shipment, so the right product reaches the right place at the right time.

😣 Your pain points

  • Demand swings — too much stock ties up cash, too little loses sales
  • Factory disruptions and quality issues you find out about too late
  • Supply chain shocks (a supplier fails, a port closes)
  • No single view across planning, production and logistics

🧰 The SAP products in your orbit

  • Plan → SAP IBP (Integrated Business Planning): demand forecasting, S&OP, inventory optimization
  • Make → SAP Digital Manufacturing: cloud MESMES = Manufacturing Execution System — software that controls and monitors what happens on the factory floor. — shop-floor control, quality, machine efficiency (OEE)
  • Move → SAP TM (Transportation Management) + EWM (Extended Warehouse Management)
  • Design → Integrated Product Development (the modern PLM)

🤖 What's new: Autonomous Supply Chain

2026's Autonomous Asset Management agents analyze past incidents, diagnose root causes, and generate pre-filled work orders with proven fixes from other sites — cutting unplanned downtime before it happens.

🔗 How it connects

Operations sits at the center of the physical-financial loop: a production order in Digital Manufacturing consumes materials (tracked in S/4HANA), planned by IBP from demand signals that may come from Ariba and customer orders. The connective tissue is, again, BTP.
