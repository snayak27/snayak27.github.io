On-Premise SAP

The original way SAP was always run: the company buys its own servers, installs SAP in its own building, and takes full responsibility for keeping it alive.

🏠 In one sentence

On-premise is owning your house outright — total freedom to knock down walls, but when the boiler breaks at 2am, it's your problem.

📦 What it actually means

"On-premise" (often shortened to on-premOn-prem = on the premises. The software lives on hardware the company owns and operates itself.) means the SAP software sits on physical servers the company bought and keeps in its own data center.

  • The company buys the hardware, installs SAP, and patches it
  • Their own IT team handles backups, security, and upgrades
  • SAP sells the software licence; everything else is the customer's job
  • The modern version is S/4HANAS/4HANA = SAP's current-generation ERP on the in-memory HANA database. on-premise; the legacy version is ECCSAP ECC = ERP Central Component, the older generation. Mainstream support ends 2027 (extended to 2030 with fees).

👍 Why a company would choose it

  • Total control — customize anything, integrate with anything, no vendor telling you what you can't change
  • Data sovereignty — some governments, banks, and defense firms are legally required to keep data on their own soil and hardware
  • Already invested — they've spent years and millions building it; ripping it out is painful

👎 The trade-offs

  • Expensive — you pay for hardware, data-center space, power, and a big IT team
  • Slow to upgrade — every new feature is a manual project
  • You carry all the risk — security, downtime, disaster recovery
  • The newest AI innovations are cloud-first — on-prem increasingly lags behind

🎬 A day in its life

A 40-year-old German auto-parts manufacturer runs SAP ECC on servers in a basement data center. Their IT team of 30 keeps it running. When SAP releases a new feature, it takes a 6-month project to adopt. They love the control — every weird process they've built over decades is baked in — but they know the 2027 support deadline is coming, and migration planning has started.

➡️ Where it's heading

On-premise is the past and (for some) the present, but not SAP's future. With ECC support ending in 2027, most on-prem customers are choosing a migration path:

  • Move to RISE with SAP (private cloud) — keep flexibility, hand off the infrastructure headache
  • Or, for simpler businesses, jump to GROW with SAP (public cloud) — standardize and modernize
