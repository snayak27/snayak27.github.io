SAP CX

The customer-facing side of SAP. While S/4HANA runs the back office, SAP CX runs the front office — marketing, selling, transacting and serving customers, all connected to the ERP behind it.

💬 The analogy

SAP CX is the storefront and the relationship manager combined — it greets the customer, sells to them, takes the order, and keeps them happy afterward, while quietly passing every order to the ERP.

❓ The problem it solves

When marketing, sales and service use separate tools, the customer gets a disjointed experience and the company has no single view of them. SAP CX connects the whole customer journey — and links it straight to S/4HANA so a sale becomes an order with no re-keying.

🧩 What's inside

  • Sales Cloud — pipeline, opportunities, field sales
  • Service Cloud — omnichannel customer support
  • Commerce Cloud — B2B/B2C e-commerce storefront
  • Customer Data Platform — unified first-party data & consent
  • Emarsys — marketing automation

(Modernized from the older "C/4HANA" branding into clean, cloud-native "V2" products.)

👤 Who uses it  ·  🔗 How it connects

  • Who: sales, marketing & service teams; the CMO and customer-facing leaders
  • Connects: orders flow into S/4HANA for fulfillment & billing; bridged by BTP

🌍 Real-world example

You browse a B2B supplier's website, add items to a cart and check out. SAP Commerce Cloud takes the order, which instantly becomes a sales order in S/4HANA — triggering delivery, invoicing and revenue recognition. Same data, no reconciliation lag, one connected experience.
