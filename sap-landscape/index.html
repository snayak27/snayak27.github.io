First — what is SAP, really?

A 30-second answer before we go anywhere.

🧠 The one-line answer

SAP is the operating system of a business. Just as Windows runs your laptop, SAP runs a company's money, people, suppliers, and products — all in one connected system, so nothing falls through the cracks.

💡 Why companies need it

Imagine a company tracking sales in one spreadsheet, stock in another, salaries in a third, and supplier bills in a fourth. Nothing agrees. Nobody trusts the numbers. That's the problem ERPERP = Enterprise Resource Planning. One central system that runs a company's core operations — finance, HR, supply chain — instead of many disconnected tools. software solves.

  • One source of truth — when a sale happens, finance, inventory, and reporting all update at once
  • SAP's flagship ERP today is called S/4HANAS/4HANA is SAP's modern ERP, built on the fast in-memory HANA database. The "4" means 4th generation.
  • Everything else SAP makes either feeds this core, extends it, or reports on it

Once you understand that SAP is the company's operating system, every other product becomes easy to place. That's what the rest of this guide does.
