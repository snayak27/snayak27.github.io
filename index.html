How I think about it

The role most are clinging to is the one I'm deliberately skipping

Three shifts are quietly closing the old SAP job:

Clean Core turns config into technical debt
AI eats the routine work
Self-service thins the translator layer

I'm not claiming I'm ready — I'm claiming I'm aimed. Most people twice my experience haven't looked up yet.

So — what am I doing about it?

Not waiting for the shift. Training onto the right side of it.

Each force that retires the old role is a force I'm deliberately building into. One concrete move against each:

vs Clean Core Learn SAP's clean way from day one BTP side-by-side extensions, Build & Joule Studio, S/4HANA standard — zero ECC config habits to unlearn.
vs AI Build the agents — don't get automated by them Shipped a 10-project agentic-AI platform: Neo4j, Chroma, MCP, Docker. I automate routine work; I'm not the routine.
vs Self-service Move up to the layer that survives Finance domain (FICO + MBA) × system design — the bilingual layer self-service can't thin.

Every shift that ends the old role is one I'm building toward — on purpose.

The window everyone's now warning about? I started walking through it before I had anything to protect.
