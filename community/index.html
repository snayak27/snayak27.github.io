The guides I wish I'd had
when I was starting out

Free, beginner-first guides for people entering SAP and tech. No jargon. No signup. No 40-tab rabbit holes. Just the clear map you needed on day one.

When I moved into SAP from a finance background, I did what every fresher in India is told to do — I went to Ameerpet, Hyderabad. Months in, I realised every institute was selling the same thing: SAP ECC — a generation behind the S/4HANA the industry had already moved to. I didn't know enough to know I was being sold the past.

Three or four months in, an SAP TechEd video randomly landed in my YouTube feed (November 2025) — and the gap hit me. The market was teaching the old world while SAP was already shipping AI agents. So I left Hyderabad, moved to Bengaluru, and started learning the only way that actually works: self-study, straight from SAP's own latest sources.

That's why this exists. The SAP training market sells what's easy to sell — old, familiar, outdated. No fresher should lose months to that. Every guide here is free, current, and grounded in official sources. I'll keep adding — one clear, honest guide at a time.
